Sound Flashlight prototype - navigating device for blind people

Biomedical group project as part of university studies. Together with the team, we designed a prototype device to assist visually impaired individuals in daily navigation.
The device—functioning like a “flashlight”—emitted ultrasonic signals that bounced off obstacles and returned to the user. Based on the sound intensity, return time, and other subtle feedback, the user could detect obstacles and orient themselves in space.

The working principle is similar to the phenomenon of echolocation.

My main responsibilities included designing the device housing and developing the electronic circuitry.
